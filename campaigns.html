16
Feb

Release of Helena Bonham-Carter portrait for Great British Oceans

Release of Helena Bonham-Carter’s much anticipated Fishlove portrait to the front page of the Evening Standard for Blue Marine Foundation's Great British Oceans campaign. The campaign is working to persuade the UK government to designate Marine Reserves in its Overseas Territories, thereby doubling the area of the globes oceans that are protected.

Press release: English Brochure
2014
01
Jun

Production of John Swannell collection

Production of John Swannell Fishlove series featuring Helena Bonham-Carter, Dame Judi Dench, Julie Christie, Zoe Wanamaker, Fiona Shaw and others. This series is due for release in Summer 2015.

2013
03
Dec

Launch of Denis Rouvre collection for Bloom

Launch of Denis Rouvre’s Fishlove collection featuring Gillian Anderson, Goldie and a number of major French celebrities for the BLOOM campaign to end destructive deep-sea trawling methods. Photographs appeared in every major European newspaper and in over 250 media outlets in the world, as well as on the Jonathan Ross TV show. An Avaaz petition launched by Bloom & Fishlove received 450,000 signatures. The campaign to achieve a European ban was lost on a technicality.

Press release: English French
21
Oct

Fishlove Exhibition in European Commission

Unprecedented Fishlove exhibition held within the European Commission in Brussels in acknowledgement of Fishlove’s pivotal role in raising awareness of the fishing crisis.

Press release: English
02
Jun

Fishlove Rome

Fishlove launch in Rome, Italy for OCEAN2012 campaign to end European subsidies for fishing fleet.

Press release: English Italian
28
May

Fishlove Paris

Release of Alan Gelati’s Melanie Laurent Fishlove portrait with an exhibition in Paris to support the OCEAN2012 campaign to reform European Common Fisheries policy. Through over 70 press hits in France alone, Fishlove helped to mobilize public support which led to “European legislators [taking] huge step toward ending overfishing by agreeing that most European Union fish stocks would be fished sustainably by 2015 and all stocks by 2020.” (source: OCEAN2012 website]

Press release: English French
2012
12
Oct

Release of Lizzy Jagger portrait for OCEAN2012

Release of Rankin’s Lizzy Jagger Fishlove portrait for the OCEAN2012 campaign to hits the front page of Evening Standard, resulting in the most prominent campaign to date. Fishlove website receives 1.5 million hits in one week alone. PR value for UK media: £410,000 (source: JBPR).

Mike Walker, of OCEAN2012, says: “It would be difficult to over-estimate the importance of Fishlove to the campaign to  end over-fishing in Europe. These images  have played a significant part in what has been achieved over the past year.”

03
Jun

Release of second Rankin collection for OCEAN2012

Release of second Rankin collection appears on cover of Sunday Times magazine on the weekend of the Queen’s Diamond Jubilee for the OCEAN2012 campaign. Major international coverage of the issue of overfishing follows, including Der Spiegel, Bild Zeitung, and a four page piece in Vanity Fair Italy.

Press release: Sunday Times
2009
01
Jun

Release of first Rankin collection for 'The End of the Line'

Release of first Rankin collection to help promote "The End of the Line" featuring Greta Scacchi, Richard E Grant, Terry Gilliam and Emilia Fox.

Greta’s photograph featured in every major UK newspaper and in media around the world, including CNN. Greta was invited into 10 Downing Street to talk to then Labour government about making fishing more sustainable. Estimated PR value: £3.5 million (source: “The End of the Line”)

The Future

Fishlove wants to continue its work by creating more Fishlove images, by exhibiting these images around the world, and by showing them on billboards in major cities. We are confident that the latest collection of photographs by John Swannell will be our most high-profile campaign yet, and we are presently looking for funders or sponsors to help us in our work to release these. We look forward to hearing from you.

“If we are now changing the face of European fisheries, it is also thanks to those who gave fish a voice. Fishlove goes even further and gives fish a face - a famous one. This brilliant idea is what civic participation is about, and is perfectly in unison with my own goal: for Europeans to buy, cook and eat sustainable fish only. I can only support it and wish Fishlove success and resonance.”

Ms Maria Damanaki European Commissioner in charge of Fisheries and Maritime Affairs
